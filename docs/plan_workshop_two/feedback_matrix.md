## 3. Workshop Feedback × Mockup

Themes raised live during the workshop presentation and feedback session mapped to the prototype.

| Feedback theme | Status in mockup | Scene(s) | What needs to change |
|---|:---:|---|---|
| **Lead with human relationships; technology enables** | ❌ | — | Prototype opens on AI-surfaced signal (Ignition). The narrative framing is intelligence-first, not relationship-first. The RM's judgement and relationship layer need to be the *opening frame*, with AI positioned as the enabler behind it |
| **Scope beyond RMs — include product colleagues** | ❌ | — | All 8 scenes are RM-facing. Product specialists, credit partners and coverage colleagues do not appear as active participants. A scene or moment showing product-to-RM collaboration or a shared deal workspace is missing |
| **One orchestrating agent, not "various agents"** | ✅ | Agent Bench | The bench has a clear orchestrator tier. Language in callouts still references "WorkIQ" / generic agents — aligning to REO explicitly would sharpen this |
| **Broader data framing: past, present, live and predicted** | ⚠️ | Ignition, Radar | Source chips shown (Companies House, CRM, etc.) but framed as "signals". No scene explicitly frames the four data horizons — historical trends, current state, live event and predicted outcome — as a unified intelligence picture |
| **Flow vs straight-through: allow for judgement checkpoints** | ⚠️ | Agent Bench | HITL accept/override exists but the overall prototype narrative is linear (8 scenes, one direction). Non-linear journeys, exploratory flows and parallel workstreams are not shown |
| **Needs vs opportunities: same thing, streamline language** | ⚠️ | Ignition, Live Moment | "Signal detected" and "opportunity surfaced" are presented as separate steps. Could be collapsed into a single continuous moment |
| **Client strategy, not bank strategy** | ⚠️ | Structure Deal, Radar | Win-probability and RoRWA framing is bank-centric. The client's strategic context (their growth, their risk, their ambition) should be the anchor, with bank economics as a consequence |
| **Beyond execution — close with the ongoing relationship** | ❌ | — | The prototype ends at Unified Platform (the deal closed). No scene shows the post-win relationship: relationship review, covenant monitoring, next opportunity in the cycle. The feedback loop is absent |
| **RM overload risk — consolidate before surfacing** | ⚠️ | Radar | Radar prioritises by High/Medium/Low but still surfaces every signal. No scene shows signal consolidation, smart filtering or "today's one thing" to prevent the RM becoming the bottleneck |
| **Service teams — should they also have access?** | ❌ | — | Not addressed. Servicing view, post-deal handoff, and ongoing monitoring for non-RM colleagues absent |
| **Direct client interaction — should clients access the platform?** | ❌ | — | All surfaces are bank-internal. Client-facing portal, self-serve or co-creation layer not considered |
| **"Outcome" vs "interaction" — favour continuity of relationship** | ⚠️ | Unified Platform | Closing scene frames a "deal outcome". Reframing around the ongoing relationship (next review, next signal, next opportunity) would better reflect the continuous cycle requested |
| **Vision language: "seamless flow" and "straight-through"** | ⚠️ | Callouts | Several callouts use "glass pipe", "seamless flow" and "end-to-end" language that the workshop flagged as automation-first. Needs softening to allow for pause, judgement and parallel paths |

### Workshop feedback summary

| Theme cluster | Coverage | Priority for workshop 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Human / relationship framing | **Not addressed** | High — reframe opening narrative |
| Scope (beyond RMs, beyond execution) | **Not addressed** | High — add product partner + post-win scene |
| Data breadth (4 horizons) | **Partial** | Medium — augment source chip language |
| Non-linear / judgement-first flow | **Partial** | Medium — show a branch or checkpoint moment |
| Language alignment (client-first, not bank-first) | **Partial** | Medium — review callout and caption copy |
| RM overload / signal consolidation | **Partial** | Medium — show "today's priority" filter |
| Client-facing / service team access | **Not addressed** | Low for next iteration — flag as future scope |

## 7. The Five Components

The five components that frame the RM journey, each broken into its constituent jobs-to-be-done.

### 1. Identify client need

- Identify high-potential prospects using internal and external signals
- Prioritise target prospects using strategic and financial thresholds
- Build a clear, up-to-date understanding of each high-potential prospect and their context
- Establish clear ownership of prospects to ensure coordinated engagement
- Identify triggers, events, and emerging needs to inform outreach

### 2. Qualify the opportunity

- Confirm client needs, priorities, and strategic objectives through interaction
- Engage prospects using the right channel, timing and context
- Gather and structure relevant information to assess opportunity value and define product proposition
- Progress opportunities through internal set up, credit and risk approval processes

### 3. Shape the proposition

- Define a clear, value-led solution aligned to client needs and bank capability (financial and non-financial products)
- Align external and internal stakeholders on solution direction and approach
- Structure and shape opportunities into clear, actionable deals

### 4. Handover origination

- Coordinate activities across internal teams and external stakeholders
- Maintain a complete, accurate view of opportunity status and history
- Provide consistent visibility of client and deal context to stakeholders

### 5. Deepen client relationship

- Maintain a deep understanding of client context, priorities and performance
- Sustain ongoing engagement to strengthen relationships and identify emerging risks or changes in performance
- Determine and execute the next best action for each client
- Ensure time-sensitive actions and client follow ups are completed and communicated clearly
- Capture and maintain accurate, up-to-date client interaction records

## 7a. Mapping the 10 Scenes to the Five Components

The 10-scene flow maps onto the five components **in flow order**, as built — a **3-2-1-2-2 split**. Each scene sits in its *true* component (the job it actually carries), so the bundles are deliberately uneven: Identify carries three scenes, Shape carries one. The flow still walks the components in sequence — Identify → Qualify → Shape → Handover → Deepen.

| Component | Primary scene bundle | Why these scenes carry it | Supporting touch |
