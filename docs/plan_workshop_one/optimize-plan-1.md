## 4. Scene-by-scene content

### 1. Opportunity ignition  *(NEW · desktop · Originate)*
Answers **"why this client, why now, why are we ahead of competitors?"** before Daisy even engages.
- Signal-led trigger: market movement / client behaviour / peer activity / portfolio performance / upcoming liquidity or funding event.
- Shows: estimated **£ value**, **urgency**, **rationale**, **timing advantage** vs competitors, and a suggested next action ("Book the review").
- Capability tag: **WorkIQ** — grounded in internal + external + unstructured signals.
- Explainability: "why this surfaced" + sources.

### 2. Activate intelligence  *(mobile · Engage — was Consent gate)*
Not an admin gate — Daisy **switching on live intelligence** for the meeting.
- Consent unlocks real-time signal detection, opportunity shaping, risk ID and automated follow-up.
- Decline path: continue manually with reduced live intelligence (RM stays in control).
- Message: consent converts the conversation into action.

### 3. Shape opportunity  *(mobile · Engage — was Live moment)*
Beyond transcription — detects intent, urgency, risk and opportunity live and **helps shape the conversation**.
- Real CIB/BCB signals: Germany expansion, liquidity pressure, FX exposure, working-capital need, timing risk. *(Backlog item 1.)*
- Each prompt shows: commercial value · why it matters · what Daisy could say next · why acting now wins (timing advantage).

### 4. Structure the deal  *(NEW · desktop · Deal shaping)*
The "winning clients" moment — shape the **best proposition**, not just a follow-up task.
- 2-3 **recommended deal structures** to compare: product combinations, indicative pricing/risk considerations, likely client fit, internal constraints, competitor positioning.
- BCB lens: working capital, lending, deposits, FX, payments, growth funding. (CIB lens noted for stretch: financing strategy, hedging, capital structure, RFP positioning.)
- Each option carries a **win-probability** and rationale. Keep it a *shaped recommendation*, not a pricing engine.

### 5. Capture momentum  *(desktop · Deal shaping — was Post-meeting summary)*
Feels like **"opportunity now progressing"**, not "summary written automatically".
- Key decisions · client intent · commercial opportunity · follow-up comms · internal actions · CRM/pipeline update.
- Shows: what happens next · who is mobilised · what's needed · what's due **today** · **risk if delayed**.
- Capability tags: **no rekeying** / fully integrated data / **glass pipe** (transparent end-to-end deal flow, visible to internal teams and brokers — vs One View today showing only pricing + approval).

### 6. Orchestrate the deal  *(desktop · Progress / Credit — was Agent bench)*
Daisy **orchestrates**; a bench of agents progresses work in the background.
- Agents from the proposal: credit readiness, product input, data gathering, pricing support, meeting prep, internal coordination, fulfilment initiation, client follow-up. *(Backlog item 2.)*
- Clear split: **agents execute; Daisy handles judgement, challenge, relationship ownership** (pulled in only where needed).
- Credit progression: prepare credit narrative, flag missing info, monitor approvals, explain risks/mitigants.
- Capability tag: **headless CRM** / unified interface (Cowork-style; link to Varshini Nimmagadda's examples).

### 7. Where you win  *(desktop · Win — was Relationship graph)*
Not a static graph — a **win strategy**: where Daisy is strong, where she's exposed, what relationships are needed to win.
- E.g. German expansion needs **Treasury** engagement → highlight missing coverage, recommend introductions.
- **Single view across the bank** *(backlog item 4)*: which RM is already on this client, who the main contact is, traction already made.
- Message: deals are won through relationship coverage as much as product capability (esp. CIB multi-stakeholder access).

### 8. Scale across portfolio  *(desktop · Scale loop — was Opportunity radar, moved to end)*
Reinforces this isn't a one-off — Daisy's **whole book is continuously monitored** for signals, risk, whitespace, next-best-action.
- Prioritises **where to spend time today** by value · urgency · likelihood to progress.
- Capability tag: **WorkIQ** data sources made explicit *(backlog item 3)* — internal systems + external market data + unstructured conversation capture.
- Closes the loop back to scene 1 (always-on origination).

## 5. Cross-cutting build items

1. **Deal-thread data model** — extend `scenario.ts` with a single `deal` object (name, value £m, per-stage state, win-probability, timing advantage, structure options, mobilised teams, credit status). Every desktop scene reads from it.
2. **Persistent deal strip** — a thin header band on **desktop scenes only** showing *deal value · stage · win-probability*, **evolving per scene** so momentum is visible (a static banner is just chrome). Where relevant it shows a downside callout (e.g. "−8% if action delayed") to stay credible and reinforce "risk if delayed". Mobile scenes (in-meeting) omit the strip.

   | Scene | Stage | Value | Win-prob |
   | --- | --- | --- | --- |
   | 1 Opportunity ignition | Identified | ~£6m (indicative) | 45% |
   | 3 Shape opportunity | Qualified | ~£8m | 58% |
   | 4 Structure the deal | Shaping | £8m | 68% |
   | 5 Capture momentum | Progressing | £8m | 72% |
   | 6 Orchestrate the deal | Credit | £8m | 78% |
   | 7 Where you win | Positioned to win | £8m | 84% |

   **Stage taxonomy:** Identified → Qualified → Shaping → Progressing → Credit → Positioned to win.
   **Deal economics (illustrative):** Avonmore Germany expansion → revolving working-capital facility + FX hedge, target ~£8m.
3. **Explainability pattern** — a reusable motif on every signal/recommendation: an **inline one-line "why" (always visible)** + an expandable **"sources"** detail (internal system · external data · conversation capture) + a confidence cue. Don't force a click to understand a card. Pair with visible **Daisy approves / overrides** actions; include **at least one explicit override moment in *Orchestrate the deal*** so "human in control / accountability" is shown, not just stated.
4. **Capability framing — generic labels, named examples** *(not new screens)*. Two buckets:
   - **Scene substance (built visually, not a chip):** *single view across the bank* **is** *Where you win*; *no-rekeying / glass pipe* **is** the integrated flow in *Capture momentum*.
   - **Framing labels (chip + plain-English one-liner):** primary label stays generic — e.g. *"always-on intelligence engine"* behind *Ignition* + *Scale*, *"unified workspace"* for *Orchestrate*. Internal/vendor names (**WorkIQ**, **Cowork**) appear as **secondary caption examples** ("e.g. WorkIQ"), so they illustrate without dominating or requiring audience familiarity.
5. **Persona swap** — James Okafor → Daisy throughout (data + UI), client elevated to the structured cross-border deal.
6. **Journey language pass** — replace "insight/summary/notes" copy with originate/shape/progress/win/£value/win-probability vocabulary.
7. **No short mode** — present the **full 8-scene journey** (decision: long version). Keyboard/arrow nav as today; all scenes shown in sequence.

## 6. Suggested sequencing (approval-gated, as before)

- **Phase A — Reframe & persona** *(low risk, high impact)*: rename scenes + chapters, Daisy persona, deal-thread data model, persistent deal strip, journey-language copy pass. No new screens.
- **Phase B — Two new scenes**: build *Opportunity ignition* (1) and *Structure the deal* (4); reorder radar to the end (8).
- **Phase C — Deepen content** *(backlog 1-4)*: real CIB/BCB signals, proposal agents in Orchestrate, WorkIQ sources in Scale, single-view-across-bank in Where-you-win.
- **Phase D — Capability tags & polish** *(backlog 5)*: glass pipe, headless CRM/Cowork, no-rekeying tags; explainability motif; win-probability/timing-advantage metrics; final responsive + PWA check.

Each phase ends with a visual review before the next begins.
