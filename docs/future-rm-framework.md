## Capture Approach — Two Options

**Flow:** `Client → System → RM → Outcome`

| | Option 1 | Option 2 |
|---|----------|----------|
| **Approach** | Passive capture + post-meeting intelligence | Live transcription + subtle prompts |
| **When insight lands** | After the meeting | In the moment + after |
| **RM experience** | No in-meeting distraction; review summary afterwards | Discreet prompts on device during the conversation |
| **Best for** | Sensitive / relationship-first meetings where presence matters | Complex or high-value discussions where real-time guidance adds value |

## Worked Example — `Client → System → RM → Outcome`

### 1. Client expresses the need
> *"Thinking of expanding into Germany next year, but cash flow timing is a concern."*

### 2. System detects
| Signal | Detected |
|---|---|
| Need / intent | Expansion into Germany |
| Risk signal | Cash flow / liquidity timing concern |
| Potential product / opportunity | Working capital solution, FX |
| Importance & urgency | High intent, near-term (next year) |
| Context linking | Industry trends, similar client situations |
| Emotion | Voice & tone (e.g. hesitation) |

### 3. System provides prompt (Option 2 — live)
1. *Expansion + liquidity concern detected — consider a working capital solution.*
2. *How peers are dealing with expansion risk + relevant products and market insights.*
3. *Client emotion: hesitation → low confidence — prompt guiding questions.*

### 4. RM acts
RM sees a subtle prompt on device and gives a guided response. System begins gathering requirements and steers the conversation.

### 5. Immediately after the meeting — auto-generated summary in CRM
- Key moments / strategic priorities
- Opportunities
- Actions
- Email summary to client

### 6. Automatic downstream flow — *no RM input required*
- CRM updated automatically (one-view, etc.)
- Follow-up email drafted (ready for approval)
- Internal tasks routed (credit / product / ops)
- Opportunity logged and prioritised

## The Agent "Bench" — RM as Orchestrator, Not Executor

A specialised team of agents that work continuously. The RM shifts from **executor → orchestrator**.

| Tier | Mode | What the agents do |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Autonomous agents** | No RM required | • Calendar & meeting prep<br>• Data gathering<br>• CRM updates<br>• Internal coordination<br>• Opportunity monitoring (signal new opps to meet quota) |
| **2. Human-in-loop agents** | Require RM approval | • Follow-up emails<br>• Opportunity recommendations<br>• Deal escalation packs<br>• Pricing recommendations |
| **3. RM-only work** | Human-based | • Strategic client conversations<br>• Relationship building<br>• Challenging discussions<br>• Commercial judgement |

> **Tier 3 is what clients expect their RM to do** — the agent bench exists to protect and expand the RM's time for it.

## Suggested Changes

1. **Make D the lens, not the last step.** Lead with "the superpower is trust" so A–C are framed as *in service of* freeing the RM. Trust currently reads as a bonus at the end rather than the organising principle.

2. **Add a "data foundation" note under Theme F.** The whole concept assumes connected CRM, comms, and market/credit signals. One line on the underlying data layer pre-empts the "this only works if the plumbing exists" question.

3. **Treat consent & compliance (A) as a first-class feature.** Show opt-in flow, on-device vs cloud storage, what's retained vs discarded. Demonstrating this builds credibility rather than inviting scrutiny.

4. **Introduce a signal-quality / suppression principle (B).** Define how the system decides what's worth interrupting the RM for, so it doesn't become another noisy dashboard.

5. **Make "rationale + source" a cross-cutting standard (B & C).** Every insight and every agent action should be explainable. This is what makes RMs willing to act on a prompt or delegate a task.

6. **Define explicit human-in-the-loop checkpoints (C).** Spell out exactly when a workflow progresses autonomously vs when it pauses for RM sign-off.

## Open Questions

- **Consent & recording:** What is our stance on recording in-person client meetings — opt-in per meeting, blanket agreement, or on-device only? How do we handle a client who declines?
- **Data residency & compliance:** Where is captured conversation data stored, for how long, and how does it satisfy MiFID II / recordkeeping obligations?
- **Signal threshold:** What defines a "moment" worth a prompt? Who tunes the sensitivity — the RM, the platform, or a central team?
- **Trust in the AI:** What level of explainability do RMs need before they'll act on a prompt or delegate work to an agent?
- **Autonomy boundaries:** Which workflows are safe to progress without RM intervention, and which always require sign-off?
- **Data foundation:** What source systems must be connected for this to work end-to-end, and what's the current state of that integration?
- **Success measures:** How do we measure "more client time" and "better conversations" — what does success look like for the pilot?
- **Scope of pilot:** Which RM segment / client book do we prototype with first to prove the concept?
