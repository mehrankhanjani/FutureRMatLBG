10-Minute Playback Guide

Audience: Senior stakeholders  ·  Tone: Inspirational — concept and possibility, not technical detail  ·  Prototype: 8 scenes, follow the arrow keys left → right

The one-sentence pitch (say this first — 20 sec)

"We're going to show you what it feels like to be a Relationship Manager at Lloyds when the bank's intelligence is working for you — not the other way around. One deal, one RM, from signal to win."

Scene-by-scene

1 · Opportunity Ignition — "The bank spots it before you do" (~70 sec)

What to say:
Daisy is a senior BCB Relationship Manager. She arrives at her desk and the platform has already flagged that two of her clients — Avonmore Components and Calderwood Engineering — have just announced an acquisition. Crucially, both companies are already Lloyds clients, but managed separately by different teams (BCB and CIB). The acquisition merges them into one group, creating a £12m opportunity — the combined group's full banking needs (financing, cash management, foreign exchange) that Lloyds can now win together, rather than lose to a competitor because the two internal teams never joined up.

The key point: this is signal-led, not relationship-memory-led. No more hoping you read the news in time. The platform knows your book, watches the market, and tells you why this client, why now, why us — with estimated value and a recommended next step already built.

Land on: "This replaces the 6am Bloomberg scan and the Monday pipeline meeting."

2 · Activate Intelligence — "Consent that feels like value, not admin" (~50 sec)

What to say:
Daisy is walking into the meeting room. On her phone, she sees the opportunity already loaded, and she can see that the client's consent is on file — meaning the platform can listen and help in real time.

Land on: "Consent is the unlock. Once it's given, the RM has an always-on co-pilot in the room."

Move quickly — this is a gateway scene, not a feature.

3 · Live Client Moment — "The conversation becomes the CRM entry" (~70 sec)

What to say:
Daisy is in the meeting with Sarah, Avonmore's CFO. Sarah says: "We need to refinance the acquisition and pull both companies' cash and FX into one place — ideally with one bank." As she speaks, the platform picks up the signal, detects the need, the risk, the urgency — and surfaces a prompt on Daisy's phone: shape a £12m group facility, move first, the integration window is open now.

Daisy stays fully present. No note-taking. No follow-up email to herself. The platform has already structured what was said into a qualified group mandate.

Land on: "The RM stays present with the client. The machine does the processing."

4 · Deal Structuring & Win Strategy — "The agent recommends — you choose" (~70 sec)

What to say:
Back at her desk, Daisy needs to shape the right proposition. The platform gives her three deal structures — lean, recommended, and stretch — with live economics: revenue, RoRWA, client fit. The agent pre-selects the recommended option, but Daisy overrides it.

This is the human-in-the-loop moment. The platform accelerates analysis; the RM still makes the call.

Land on: "We're not replacing RM judgement — we're giving it better raw material."

5 · Post-Meeting Momentum — "The meeting writes itself" (~60 sec)

What to say:
Two minutes after the meeting ends, the CRM is already updated. Key moments, deal components, actions with owners and deadlines, and a draft client email — all generated from the conversation.

Three things to name here:

  • Glass pipe — conversation becomes structured data, transparently
  • Headless CRM — Cowork writes the record; Daisy never touches the CRM UI
  • No re-keying — captured once, flows everywhere
Land on: "The bane of every RM's life — CRM admin — just disappears."

6 · Agent Bench & Orchestration — "A team that never sleeps" (~70 sec)

What to say:
While Daisy is on her next client call, a bench of agents is working in the background: drafting the credit pack, running pricing analysis, aligning with the CIB coverage team, preparing the indicative proposal.

She can see exactly what's running, what's waiting for her approval, and what only she can do. When the pricing agent recommends +2.15%, she overrides it to +2.40% — her call, her relationship.

Land on: "The RM's leverage multiplies. Same Daisy, eight things happening at once."

7 · Portfolio Opportunity Radar — "Scale the play across the book" (~60 sec)

What to say:
The Avonmore win is one deal. But the same M&A signal pattern exists across Daisy's whole book. The radar surfaces every client where the same play could work — ranked by value and urgency, with a reason to act now.

Land on: "One good idea, scaled across 40 relationships — without hiring 40 more RMs."

8 · Unified Platform — "One bank, one view" (~50 sec)

What to say:
The final scene shows what the platform looks like at its best: client, deal and workflow visible end-to-end — across BCB and CIB — in one place. The Avonmore group mandate, from the M&A signal all the way to positioned-to-win, on a single screen.

Land on: "This is what it looks like when the bank stops working in silos."

Closing (30 sec)

"What you've just seen is one deal — but the same intelligence engine works across every RM, every client, every product. The question isn't whether this is possible. It's how fast we want to move."

If asked: "That's exactly the right question for the next conversation — this session is about the vision."
