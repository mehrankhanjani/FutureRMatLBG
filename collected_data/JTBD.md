# Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) — CIB & BCB Relationship Manager Journey

---

## 1. Prospecting

**Description:** Understand where opportunity exists

### Core Jobs to Be Done
- Identify high-potential prospects
- Confirm prospects align to strategic and financial thresholds
- Prioritise pipeline of target prospects using internal and external signals
- Build a clear, up-to-date understanding of each high-potential prospect and detailed context
- Establish clear ownership of new-to-bank prospects to ensure coordinated engagement
- Identify triggers, events, and emerging needs to inform outreach

### Pain Points
- Fragmented prospect intelligence (e.g. client/market insight) limits visibility and targeting
- Manual prospecting reduces efficiency and RM capacity
- Inconsistent prospecting approaches lead to variable performance
- Opportunity identification is more reactive than insight-led
- Growth is heavily influenced by individual RM relationships

## 2. Needs Analysis

**Description:** Understand client requirements and shape solutions

### Core Jobs to Be Done
- Engage prospects using the right channel, timing and context
- Explore value-add propositions outside of financial product proposition
- Gather and structure relevant client information to assess opportunity value and define product proposition
- Initial introduction with product partners to define potential solution direction aligned to client needs and bank capabilities
- Confirm client needs, priorities, and strategic objectives through initial client interaction
- Capture and share visibility of outreach activity across colleagues

### Pain Points
- Client insights are not consistently captured or reused
- Ownership and relationship history lack clear visibility
- Client and opportunity data is fragmented across teams
- Financial and credit assessment remains manual and time-intensive

## 3. Origination and Pipeline

**Description:** Structure, agree and progress opportunities

### Core Jobs to Be Done
- Structure and shape opportunities into clear, actionable deals
- Progress opportunities by completing required internal set up and securing approval through credit and risk processes
- Coordinate internal product expertise throughout origination
- Provide a complete view of client, opportunity and history to adjacent teams, senior stakeholders and interested parties
- Coordinate activities across multiple teams and stakeholders

### Pain Points
- Deal progression is slowed by fragmented processes, isolated systems/platforms and lengthy approvals
- No single view of pipeline or end-to-end deal status
- Internal setup processes introduce delays
- Opportunity and client context is not consistently carried across systems
- CRM being perceived as an additional administrative burden that is not adding value

## 4. Ongoing Client Relationship

**Description:** Grow and manage client relationship

### Core Jobs to Be Done
- Build and maintain a deep understanding of each client's business, context and priorities
- Maintain ongoing client engagement to strengthen relationship and identify emerging risks or changes in performance
- Determine and execute the right next action for each client based on understanding of engagement, context and priorities
- Ensure time-sensitive actions and client follow ups are completed and communicated clearly
- Capture and share timely, accurate client interaction and insights to maintain a reliable up-to-date view of the relationship

### Pain Points
- No consolidated view of client activity and relationship health
- Ongoing interactions are not consistently captured or shared
- Relationship insights are not easily accessible or actionable
- Client knowledge remains dependent on individual RMs
- Lack of proactive next best actions to support RMs to run their day
