# Workshop Principles: What Does Great Look Like?

---

## 1. Understanding What a Client Really Needs

> *How do we build a deep, real-time, validated view of client needs?*

- **Validated & benchmarked needs** — Client needs are confirmed with reasoning and compared against industry/sector peers and LBG's own portfolio
- **Proactive & predictive intelligence** — Real-time analysis surfaces at-risk flags, lost deal reasons, trends, and warm prospecting leads (including non-clients affected by external events)
- **360° insight** — Internal data, external signals, competitor positioning, and market trends all feed a single, unified picture
- **Agent-led discovery** — Always-on AI agents (e.g. 'Jack'-style) conduct continuous discovery between RM touchpoints, ensuring no moment is missed
- **Actionable nudges** — Predictive alerts guide RMs to the right action at the right moment, enabling smarter, faster client conversations

---

## 2. Knowing This Is Worth Pursuing

> *How do we quickly qualify whether an opportunity is real and worth our effort?*

- **Indicative approvals at point of need** — When a client need is identified, the system provides immediate indicative outputs: debt capacity, default grade, likelihood of approval
- **Propensity & appetite scoring** — Credit, product, geographic, and sector appetite are assessed automatically to prioritise the right opportunities
- **Soft-search capability** — RMs can explore opportunities without triggering formal processes, building confidence before commitment
- **Minimal friction at capture** — Only the bare minimum data is collected at opportunity entry to encourage in-the-moment logging
- **AI-assisted qualification** — AI supports, not replaces, the RM's judgement — providing clear, unambiguous gateways to progression with human-in-the-loop validation
- **Red-line automation** — Hard disqualifiers (e.g. jurisdiction or credit rules) are automatically flagged so RMs aren't pursuing non-starters

---

## 3. Shaping the Best Solution

> *How do we build the right proposition, quickly, and with confidence?*

- **AI-suggested & iterable propositions** — Opportunities are surfaced proactively and can be rapidly iterated with AI support, grounded in client circumstances
- **Benchmarked & multi-product thinking** — Solutions are not restricted to a single product; a range of options is presented so product specialists can design the right outcome
- **Client file as single source of truth** — A shared, accessible client record ensures all service lines work from the same information
- **Product-to-RM proactivity** — Product teams surface relevant options to RMs (reverse proactivity), keeping RMs informed of new products and their suitability
- **Benefit framing** — Solutions clearly show value to the client, the bank, and the RM's portfolio
- **Confidence in delivery** — Propositions are supported by credit models and product logic, including bespoke pricing within RM delegations
- **Portfolio-level strategy** — Solutions can reflect overarching portfolio preferences (e.g. RWA optimisation) and internal flexibility requirements

---

## 4. Managing Risk & Approvals Without Slowing Progress

> *How do we keep compliance and credit integrated into the deal flow, not bolted on?*

- **Approvals embedded in workflow** — No email chains; approval steps are part of the deal journey itself
- **Data captured once, used everywhere** — Information collected at client interaction feeds all downstream approval requirements (credit, KYC, product-specific)
- **Proactive data freshness** — Owners are prompted to keep data current; the system monitors relevance automatically
- **AI-reviewed submissions** — AI pre-reviews approval requests and provides suggested outcomes to both RM and Credit before human review
- **In-life monitoring** — Ongoing sensitivity analysis, covenant tracking, and obligation monitoring flag issues before they escalate
- **Automated triage** — Cases are routed and progressed automatically; no human required to push things along
- **Business strategy visibility** — Revenue implications and balance sheet impacts are visible alongside client recommendations so decisions align with broader strategy

---

## 5. RM–Client Interactions That Feel Joined Up

> *How do we make every client touchpoint feel personalised, informed, and seamless?*

- **Full interaction history, any channel** — RMs have a real-time view of every client touchpoint — call centre, digital, RM-led, or outreach — with no gaps
- **Personalised, timely outreach** — Predictive analytics prompt RMs to reach out at the right moments with tailored, relevant advice
- **Silent AI, RM voice** — AI drafts communications in the RM's tone of voice; the RM approves and sends. Clients experience the RM, not the AI
- **No repeat information** — Clients never need to repeat themselves; context travels with them across every touchpoint
- **Collaboration on complex deals** — Multiple RMs across specialties and geographies can work a single deal with full shared context and defined approval/judgement points
- **Email & outreach agent** — Intuitive AI-led drafting for proactive client outreach, reviewed and owned by the RM

---

## 6. Moving Forward Without Rework or Delays

> *What foundations must be in place to eliminate friction from the process?*

- **Enter data once** — Information is captured once and automatically distributed to all who need it — no re-keying, no duplication
- **Seamless onboarding, any complexity** — The same data model supports clients from SME to complex CIB structures without manual bridges
- **Client-only asks** — We only approach the client for information we genuinely cannot obtain ourselves; and when we do, it is a single, coordinated ask
- **Intuitive process with AI coaching** — The journey is self-explanatory for standard cases; a coach surfaces guidance at points of complexity
- **One-place tracking** — All workflows, SLAs, and onboarding stages are visible in a single view so everyone knows exactly where things stand

## Vision Statements

### North Star

**Originators are the trusted advisor and value multiplier** — interpreting signals, shaping client strategy, bringing the whole bank to the client, with judgment, trust, and accountability.

**Client and originator needs are continuously anticipated and orchestrated by intelligent agents**, powered by real-time data across the ecosystem.

**Every client outcome is delivered as a seamless flow from insight to execution** across the client lifecycle — where risk is embedded, duplication is eliminated, and process is straight through.

---

### 3-Year Vision

The journey runs in real time with agents anticipating originator and client needs, orchestrating end-to-end execution with control by design. Originators focus on interpreting signals, implementing client strategy and leading critical moments with full accountability and frictionless processes.

---

### 12-Month Vision

A single origination journey, with data-driven insights and agents that are embedded into daily workflows, with every interaction and opportunity captured, workflows partially automated, reducing manual effort and shifting time toward client interactions, growth and better-informed judgement.

## North Star — Workshop Feedback

> *Live reactions and suggested refinements from the group*

### What Resonated
- The closing sentence ("every client outcome delivered as a seamless flow...") — keep as-is
- "Continuously anticipated and orchestrated by intelligent agents" — strong, widely liked
- The overall shape of the first two sentences

### What Needs to Change

| Theme | Feedback |
|---|---|
| **Human relationships first** | Originators build relationships; technology enables them — this needs to come through more clearly. The human layer is as important as the decisive/value layer |
| **Scope beyond RMs** | Remove the implicit focus on Relationship Managers only; product colleagues are equally part of this vision |
| **One orchestrating agent** | Rather than "various agents", the preference is for a single agent that synthesises and presents back to the originator |
| **Broader data framing** | "Real-time data" is too narrow — the vision should cover all data: historical, current, live, and predicted |
| **Flow vs. straight-through** | "Single seamless flow" and "no gap between decision and action" may not reflect reality — some situations warrant pause, review, or spin-off flows |
| **Needs vs. opportunities** | Client needs *are* opportunities — avoid treating them as separate concepts; streamline the language |
| **Client strategy, not bank strategy** | Originators shape the *client's* strategy and bring the best of the bank to them — they don't direct the bank's strategy |
| **Beyond execution** | The vision should extend past execution to the ongoing relationship and feedback loop |

### Suggested Direction for Revision
- Lead with the human relationship and the enabling role of technology
- Frame originators as the **human and trusted layer** — not just the decisive or value layer
- Broaden data references to past, present, live, and predicted
- Replace "straight-through" language with something that allows for judgement checkpoints and parallel flows
- Close with the idea of a continuous cycle: insight → action → relationship → insight

## Open Questions for the Next Iteration

> *Unresolved points and challenges raised by the group*

### Scope & Access
- **Service teams** — Do service colleagues (beyond originators and product) also need access to insights and the client picture? If so, how does their view differ?
- **Direct client interaction** — Should clients themselves be able to interact directly with the platform, or is the RM always the intermediary?

### Signal Management & AI Role
- **From signal to action** — What happens between a signal being detected and an action being taken? Is the AI consolidating, ranking, and prioritising signals before surfacing anything to the originator — or is every signal surfaced raw?
- **RM overload risk** — If too many signals and decisions are routed through the RM as the human-in-the-loop, the RM becomes the bottleneck. How do we prevent the human layer from becoming the blocker?

### Language & Framing
- **"Outcome" vs. "interaction"** — Should the vision talk about *outcomes* (implying finality) or *interactions* (implying continuity and relationship)? The latter may better reflect the ongoing nature of the RM–client relationship
- **Non-linear journeys** — The current framing implies a linear flow. How do we account for exploratory, iterative, or parallel workstreams that don't follow a straight path?

### Research & Intelligence
- **Benchmarking & research support** — How do we help originators research, compare, and benchmark opportunities using the best available external data and LBG's own insight — going beyond nudges to genuinely augmenting their analytical capability?
